Tourist Spots in Switzerland

1. Zunich, Switzerland

With its stunning Alpine scenery and cosy cafes, Zurich offers a delightful autumn escape. Hike up Uetliberg for panoramic views of the city and Lake Zurich, stroll through the Botanical Garden‘s vibrant foliage, and explore the charming streets of the Old Town. The red and orange autumnal hues are sure to paint the picturesque Swiss landscape with beautiful colours. It is no wonder Zurich makes a wonderful escape into one of the cosiest destinations in autumn!

2. Lucerne, Switzerland

One of Switzerland's prettiest cities, Lucerne is a medieval Swiss town known for its beautiful cobalt lake, well-preserved architecture, iconic wooden bridge, 19th century chapels, fabulous Swiss views and delicious Alpine cheese.Lucerne offers many sights and is the perfect hub for excursions to the region around Lake Lucerne. One of Europe’s oldest covered bridges serves as its centerpiece, and fresco-adorned historic houses line the streets, but it’s also home to the cutting-edge KKL, a concert hall and art gallery.

3. Rhine Falls, Switzerland

The Rhine Falls, the biggest waterfall in Europe, offer the visitors a spectacular sight! 17,000 years ago this impressive wonder of nature was formed and is now the main attraction for countless visitors from all over the world. Over a width of 150 metres and a height of 23 metres, an average of 700,000 litres of water cascade over the rocks every second. A boat ride to the famous rock in the middle of the Falls and the short walk to the top, is an unforgettable experience for every visitor.

4. Schloss Oberhofen, Switzerland

Oberhofen Castle with its medieval keep and lake tower is located on the shore of Lake Thun. The castle, which dates back to the early 13th century, houses a living museum. The large castle park is supposed to be one of the most magnificent in the region of the Alps.

5. Binntal Valley, Switzerland

World-class classical music and outstanding cuisine in a mountain village, exceptional architecture in an attractive landscape – the Binntal valley is full of surprises, not to mention unspoilt nature from the mountain peaks down to the edge of the villages and all the way to the Rotten (Rhone) river.
